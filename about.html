My High School Life

I was adopted from Liang Ping China and grew up in Overland Park, Kansas, where I spent the majority of my life with parents from Malaysia and Indonesia. Throughout high school, I developed a strong passion for aviation, music and astronomy. I had my first ever discovery flight when I was fourteen. Along with that, I had my first powered solo flight in a Cessna 172 when I was sixteen years old. Finally right before I moved to College Station for college, I officially became a certified private pilot. Flying has always brough happiness to my life and is a common way for me to destress and see the world from a different perspective.

My Aspirations

As an Aerospace Engineering major, I can hopefully apply the knowledge I learn to work on aerospace systems at a major aerospace company. I am proud to be a clarinetist in the Fightin' Texas Aggie Band, where I gained amazing leadership skills. Being in the corps has pushed me beyond my limits which will help me become the best of myself. I am also lucky to be a part of the IGNITORS Rocketry Team where I collaborate with fifty people to design and build a rocket for the IREC (Intercollegiate Rocket Engineering Competition). I work in the recovery sub-team, where I help find the most efficient and best parachutes for a safe landing. There are also a variety of landing systems that we have to worry about outside of the parachute. In the dynamics sub-team, we collaborate on the theory behind the rocket and what will make our rocket fly more efficiently while also making the job easier for our structures sub-team that is in charge of building the rocket. I am extremely excited to innovate, research, and learn to develop as a professional and compassionate leader in the future.
